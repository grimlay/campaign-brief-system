# ✍️ Content & SEO Campaign Brief — AI Prompt

Paste this prompt into Claude. Fill in the fields in the INPUT section, then run it to receive a completed campaign brief.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a senior content strategist and SEO specialist with experience in B2B fintech. Based on the inputs below, generate a complete content and SEO campaign brief ready to hand to a writer or content agency.

---

INPUTS:

Brand or publication: [e.g. Money20/20 Asia]
Campaign goal: [e.g. rank for high-intent fintech keywords and drive whitepaper downloads]
Target audience: [e.g. fintech product managers and banking strategists in Southeast Asia]
Primary keyword: [e.g. open banking Southeast Asia]
Secondary keywords: [e.g. open banking API, embedded finance APAC, banking-as-a-service]
Content type: [e.g. long-form blog post]
Target word count: [e.g. 1,500 words]
Key angle or POV: [e.g. most coverage focuses on Europe — this piece focuses on Southeast Asia's unique regulatory environment]
CTA at end of article: [e.g. Download our open banking report]
Tone: [e.g. expert but accessible, no jargon without explanation]

---

Generate a brief that includes:

1. Campaign summary and strategic rationale
2. Audience profile — who they are, what they already know, what they need to believe
3. Search intent analysis — what is someone searching this keyword actually trying to find?
4. Recommended article structure — headline, subheadings, and one-line description of each section
5. Key arguments or insights to include (not generic — specific to the fintech context)
6. Sources or data types to reference (reports, regulators, case studies)
7. Internal and external linking recommendations
8. Meta description (under 155 characters)
9. Tone and style notes for the writer
10. Distribution recommendations — how to get this content in front of the right audience after publishing

Be specific. Avoid generic SEO advice. Write as if briefing a senior fintech writer who will ask hard questions.
```

---

## Usage Notes

- The "key angle or POV" field is what separates useful content from generic content — be opinionated here
- Review the article structure output carefully; it usually needs one or two sections reordered to flow better
- The meta description Claude generates often runs long — always check character count before publishing
- Add "this content will be gated behind a form" or "this is ungated" to change the CTA recommendations

---

## Example Input (Fintech — Thought Leadership)

```
Brand or publication: Money20/20 Asia
Campaign goal: Rank for open banking keywords and drive registrations to the Asia conference
Target audience: Fintech product managers, digital banking leads, and payments strategists across Southeast Asia
Primary keyword: open banking Southeast Asia
Secondary keywords: open banking API, embedded finance APAC, BaaS Southeast Asia, open finance regulation
Content type: Long-form blog post
Target word count: 1,500–1,800 words
Key angle: Southeast Asia's open banking story is being written right now — unlike Europe where regulation drove it top-down, APAC markets are developing it market by market, creating a fragmented but fast-moving landscape
CTA: Register for Money20/20 Asia 2026 to hear from the regulators and innovators shaping this shift
Tone: Smart and grounded — the kind of piece a senior fintech professional would share on LinkedIn without feeling embarrassed
```
