# 📧 Email Campaign Brief — AI Prompt

Paste this prompt into Claude. Fill in the fields in the INPUT section, then run it to receive a completed campaign brief.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a senior B2B marketing strategist specialising in fintech. Based on the inputs below, generate a complete email campaign brief. The brief should be structured, specific, and actionable — ready to hand to a copywriter or email specialist without further clarification.

---

INPUTS:

Product or event: [e.g. Money20/20 Asia 2026 — annual fintech conference in Bangkok]
Campaign goal: [e.g. drive early bird registrations before pricing deadline]
Target audience: [e.g. payments professionals and banking executives in Southeast Asia]
Funnel stage: [e.g. mid-funnel — aware of the event but not yet registered]
Key audience pain point: [e.g. unsure if the content is senior enough for their level]
Primary CTA: [e.g. Register now]
Urgency or incentive: [e.g. Standard Pass at USD 999, price increases after Friday]
Number of emails: [e.g. 3-email sequence]
Tone: [e.g. professional, peer-to-peer, not corporate or salesy]

---

Generate a brief that includes:

1. Campaign summary (2–3 sentences)
2. Audience profile and funnel stage rationale
3. Core message and up to 3 supporting points
4. What NOT to say or do in this campaign
5. Email sequence outline — for each email: suggested send timing, subject line direction, narrative focus, and CTA
6. Recommended tone and voice notes for the copywriter
7. Success metrics — what does good look like for this campaign?

Be specific. Avoid generic marketing language. Write as if you are briefing an experienced copywriter who will push back if the brief is vague.
```

---

## Usage Notes

- The more specific your inputs, the better the brief — don't leave fields vague
- "Key audience pain point" is the most important field; it shapes the entire brief
- After generating, review point 4 ("What NOT to say") carefully — Claude sometimes misses nuances that only you would know
- Use the output as a starting point; add internal context (budget, approval process, brand restrictions) before sharing with your team

---

## Example Input (Fintech — B2B Event)

```
Product or event: Money20/20 Asia 2026 — annual fintech conference, Bangkok, April 21–23
Campaign goal: Convert warm leads to paid registrations before early bird pricing ends
Target audience: Payments and banking professionals in Southeast Asia who have opened previous event emails but not registered
Funnel stage: Mid-funnel — aware, not yet convinced
Key audience pain point: Not sure if the speaker lineup is senior or relevant enough to justify the trip
Primary CTA: Register Now
Urgency: Standard Pass at USD 999 — price increases after Friday
Number of emails: 3-email sequence over 2 weeks
Tone: Credible, warm, peer-to-peer — like a colleague recommending an event, not a sales pitch
```
